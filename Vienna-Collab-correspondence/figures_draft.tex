\caption{Molecular alignment of gas-phase CS2 \textbf{(a)} and CS2 molecules embedded in He droplets \textbf{(b)} to the same phase-stabilized centrifuge. The centrifuge drives the rotation of CS2 in the liquid helium environment past the centrifugal wall at 20 GHz. The size of the error bar at each probe delay corresponds to the standard error of the mean of the average $\langle\cos^2\theta_{\mathrm{2D}}\rangle(t)$ over several scans.}

\caption{STFT of the experimental (left panel) and theoretical (right panel) $\langle\cos^2\theta_{\mathrm{2D}}\rangle(t)$ signal of molecular rotation in helium droplets of CS\textsubscript{2} \textbf{(a)} and OCS with a higher centrifuge acceleration ($\approx 0.34$ GHz/ps), and OCS with a lower centrifuge acceleration ($\approx 0.26$ GHz/ps). In each case, rotation persists at frequencies above the molecule's centrifugal wall. Note that the centrifuge used in \textbf{(b)} decelerates in the beginning of the pulse before reaching 0 GHz, and accelerating. This is reflected in the corresponding spectrogram \textbf{b} from about -250 ps to -150 ps.}
